📚 3 rabbit holes today

🤖 How AI agents actually pay each other in 2026
The mechanics are basically live, and the argument has moved from "can agents pay" to "should they, and through whose rails." The default settlement rail people name is x402 - an HTTP 402 "Payment Required" revival, not a new blockchain: an agent hits a paid endpoint, gets a 402, signs a USDC payment, retries, gets the data. No accounts, no API keys, no human in the loop. Median payment sizes are sub-dollar ($0.34 on x402, $0.08 on Stripe/Tempo's MPP) - a scale legacy card rails can't touch at 2.9% + $0.30, which is the whole reason this exists. It's a layered stack, not a protocol war: authorization (Google's AP2/ACP mandates prove a human granted spend authority), commerce (ACP powers ChatGPT checkout), settlement (x402/MPP move the value). Wallets now ship as MCP toolkits, so "I gave my AI a wallet" is a two-minute demo. The unsolved parts, per builders running live endpoints: trust, recourse, and the centralization tell - every x402 tx settles in USDC on Base, so Circle can freeze and Coinbase can gate the "neutral money for software."

🎨 Manga/anime artists vs AI image models
It's an authorship fight, not an anti-AI one. When X shipped an AI "edit image" button, Dr. STONE artist Boichi paused posting his work there - while explicitly saying "I believe in its future." The demand crystallized into three words used by both a top mangaka and Patreon's CEO this month: credit, compensation, consent. The real leverage turned out to be infrastructure, not takedowns - Patreon + Cloudflare now block training crawlers at the network level, and Cloudflare starts blocking them by default across millions of sites in September. "Human-made" has hardened into a certification arms race (Ahoy Comics' "100% Human-Made" stamp; a Ghost in the Shell remake announced as fully hand-drawn), though NPR warns the badges are fragmenting into "label blindness." Enforcement against studios is fan-driven catch-and-apologize (WIT Studio redrawing AI backgrounds), not courts. For style mimicry, artists point to Glaze (cloak your style) and Nightshade (poison scrapers) as the go-to toolkit.

💸 "Everyone is a token arbitrageur"
The pitch: every knowledge worker is a token-processing machine, and for the first time human and machine cognitive labor sit on the same balance sheet, in the same unit. Winners arbitrage - spend tokens where they pay off, skip them where they don't. The purest test case was Meta's internal "Claudeonomics" leaderboard ranking 6,000 staff by token usage (73.7 trillion tokens in ~30 days, ~$50K/head) - which rewarded volume over output, so Meta capped it and pivoted from "tokenmaxxing" to token-managing. The optimist's endgame is the one-person unicorn (solo founders now ~36% of new ventures), but the skeptics have the harder numbers this month: a Feb 2026 NBER study found 90% of firms report no measurable AI productivity impact, and Goldman found no economy-wide effect outside a ~30% boost in just coding and customer support. The named mechanism: a "verification tax" - ~40% of self-reported time savings gets eaten by rework and babysitting the model. Big task-level gains, near-zero firm-level ROI, is the whole debate.
