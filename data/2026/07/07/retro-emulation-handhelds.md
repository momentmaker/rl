What I learned:

**The retro scene right now is split between two philosophies: cheap Android handhelds for people who just want to play, and FPGA hardware for people who care about how the games run.** On the handheld side, [Retro Handhelds](https://retrohandhelds.gg/) is tracking a steady drumbeat of releases — the Anbernic RG 55G1, the Retroid Pocket Flip 3, and the PULSE Android handheld with hardware performance controls. These are the mass-market entry points: sub-$100-to-$200 devices running RetroArch/Android that "just work." The FPGA side is a different world, and it's where the passion (and the drama) lives.

**[MiSTer FPGA](https://x.com/PixelCNinja/status/2073844362304524520) is having a busy month — core releases are coming out constantly.** [@PixelCNinja](https://x.com/PixelCNinja/status/2073844362304524520)'s "Retro & Related News" roundups logged a flood of new cores in the last week alone: Psikyo SH2, an SH3 Verilog core, Boogie Wings, Paperboy, KLAX, Double Dribble, and a MiSTer Companion iOS app. New standalone hardware is arriving too — [@StreamTheSteam](https://x.com/StreamTheSteam/status/2074187605638254979) took delivery of a "SuperStation Founders plus Dock," part of a wave of MiSTer-compatible boxes aimed at people who don't want to assemble a DE10-Nano rig themselves. Not all of it ships clean: [@jd_sauvage](https://x.com/jd_sauvage/status/2073107951481491585) noted that Retro Remake's MiSTer FPGA PS1 clone's disk drive "doesn't work out of the box."

**The scene is arguing with itself about two things: AI-generated FPGA cores and whether any of this hardware is even necessary.** The [@PixelCNinja](https://x.com/PixelCNinja/status/2074206748231999667) roundup flagged "Jotego's Statement on AI Use for FPGA Cores" — a live tension about whether LLMs should be writing the Verilog that makes these cores accurate. And [@pr4m0d](https://x.com/pr4m0d/status/2073004842457457129) put the skeptic's case bluntly: "the games are already playable today on MAME, retroarch, etc. I am starting to wonder if people forgot about these... There is no reason for someone to use AI to make a broken thing that's been playable for the better part of the last 20 years." [@MrMisterMini](https://x.com/MrMisterMini/status/2073841454770794693) extended that to the whole hobby: modern retro hardware is "a nice collection of dust catchers and boxes but it's the games that count... the modern value is artificial." The counter-camp, like [@M141A22883](https://x.com/M141A22883/status/2072788345781092526), are "huge believers" in MiSTer and its Optical Drive Emulator precisely because emulation-vs-hardware fidelity is the point.

**The legal weather is worsening in the background.** The same news cycle carried "SONY Deleting PSN Accounts" and "PS5 Jailbreak Interest Rising" — a reminder that as official platforms get more hostile to preservation and account ownership, interest in jailbreaking and hardware emulation ticks up in response.

KEY PATTERNS from the research:

1. **Two markets, not one.** Cheap Android handhelds (Anbernic, Retroid, PULSE) optimize for convenience; FPGA (MiSTer, Analogue Pocket, SuperStation) optimizes for cycle-accuracy. They rarely compete for the same buyer.
2. **MiSTer core output is the scene's heartbeat** — the volume of weekly core releases is the clearest signal the FPGA community is thriving, not fading.
3. **AI-written cores are the new fault line**, with respected core authors publicly stating positions on whether LLM-generated Verilog belongs in accuracy-critical hardware.
4. **"The games, not the boxes"** is the recurring skeptic refrain — a healthy internal check against collector/hardware fetishism.
5. **Platform hostility is a tailwind** — PSN account deletions and jailbreak interest push more people toward preservation-minded emulation.
