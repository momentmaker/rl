What I learned:

**The mothership is on life support, but the machines outlived it** - The single biggest story in the last 30 days is not a new product - it's that Precious Plastic itself is in crisis. The One Army team behind it now has no workspace, is fully remote, was forced out of its Eindhoven building after Chrome-6 chemicals were found, and by its own admission has roughly six months of runway left, per [Precious Plastic](https://www.preciousplastic.com/news/problems-in-precious-plastic). Their stated root problem is telling for anyone asking "is this worth it": they never found a business model that funds the team without competing with their own community. The open-source repo that runs the community platform, [onearmy/community-platform](https://github.com/onearmy/community-platform), is still live at 1.4K stars with 26 open issues, but a project like this "needs many people caring for it to stay alive."

**What people actually make is homeware, sheets, and building blocks - not a living** - The honest answer to "what do they make" is small, flat, and decorative: recycled-HDPE sheets, tiles, beams, flowerpots, coasters, clocks, jewelry, and carabiners sold on the [Precious Plastic Bazar](https://bazar.preciousplastic.com/products/). The most ambitious 2026 direction is construction: at CES 2026, WES-Tec's ECOCCUBE turned 100% recycled waste plastic into building blocks for flood-prone regions and took an Innovation Award, per [Interesting Engineering](https://interestingengineering.com/ces-2026/recycled-plastic-construction-blocks) - a sign the "blocks from waste plastic" idea is going professional even as the DIY movement wobbles.

**Local workspaces are the part that's actually alive** - The counter-narrative to the org's decline is that individual makerspaces keep running. A Precious Plastic "Re-Makerspace" is operating in Breckenridge, Colorado as of June 2026 with a full machine set, per [Breck Create](https://breckcreate.org/education/precious-plastic-initiative). The model has quietly shifted from a central movement to a scatter of local, often arts- or education-funded shops.

**"Worth it" depends entirely on why you're doing it** - The community's honest verdict: profitable at home, basically no. Educational, artistic, or hobby value, yes. [Recycling Today](https://www.recyclingtoday.org/blogs/news/diy-plastic-recycling-at-home-with-a-mini-shredder) frames DIY shredding as worthwhile for hobbyists and educators but not at scale, and flags the practical traps - PVC (#3) and polystyrene (#6) give off toxic fumes, and shredders run 80-100 dB and need real ventilation. On [r/recycling](https://www.reddit.com/r/recycling/comments/1tyegje/how_many_times_plastic_can_really_be_recycled/), the recurring reality check is that plastic only survives a few recycle loops before quality collapses.

**The economics are stacked against the little guy** - The macro backdrop makes the home case even harder: recycled plastic has to beat virgin resin on cost and quality, and transport inefficiency (small bales vs 190,000-lb railcars of virgin resin) keeps recycled cost-per-pound high, per [Resource Recycling](https://resource-recycling.com/plastics-recycling-update-magazine/2026/02/19/how-will-2026-unfold-for-plastics-recycling/). The one place home effort clearly pays off is upstream: household pre-sorting pushes national recycling rates above 50% in the Netherlands, Belgium and Germany, per [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01823-y) - the value is cleaner feedstock, not garage profit.

KEY PATTERNS from the research:
1. The central Precious Plastic org is in an existential funding crisis with ~6 months runway, no viable business model - per [Precious Plastic](https://www.preciousplastic.com/news/problems-in-precious-plastic)
2. Typical maker output is flat/decorative goods - sheets, tiles, beams, flowerpots, coasters - sold on the [Bazar](https://bazar.preciousplastic.com/products/), rarely a full income
3. Recycled-plastic construction blocks (ECOCCUBE at CES 2026) are the credible scale-up path, per [Interesting Engineering](https://interestingengineering.com/ces-2026/recycled-plastic-construction-blocks)
4. Home recycling is worth it for education/art/hobby, not profit; watch fumes (PVC/PS) and noise - per [Recycling Today](https://www.recyclingtoday.org/blogs/news/diy-plastic-recycling-at-home-with-a-mini-shredder)
5. Household pre-sorting beats garage recycling on impact - cleaner feedstock lifts national rates past 50%, per [Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-026-01823-y)
