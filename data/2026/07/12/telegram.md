📚 3 rabbit holes today

🧠 Can controlling AI compute actually slow frontier AI?
The whole US export-control regime assumes a top-down stack: restrict the chips, and you throttle the models. This month the assumption is visibly wobbling. A ~$25M Chinese model (GLM-5.2) landed within a point of frontier labs that spent orders of magnitude more - so "reaching the frontier is prohibitively expensive" may just be a story US valuations are priced on. Controls also leak badly: Epoch AI estimates 290K-1.6M H100-equivalents were smuggled into China through end-2025 (~a third of its compute), which pushed Congress to pass a Chip Security Act embedding tracking into chips. And they're backfiring into a domestic industry - DeepSeek is building its own silicon, Huawei is herding 2,000+ firms toward 70% self-sufficiency by 2028. The "controls work" camp isn't conceding: China still owns just ~5% of leading-chip compute despite all the smuggling. The sharpest take is that compute was never the real lever - the physical chokepoint is the EUV/foundry supply chain crossing 70 borders, and controls now function more as trade-alignment leverage (easing for UAE while tightening on China) than a clean throttle.

🎨 Tailwind fatigue - and what devs actually move to
In 2026 the Tailwind debate stopped being about taste and became a survival question: Adam Wathan reportedly cut ~75% of the technical team after an ~80% sales drop, because AI now emits utility classes on demand and gutted the paid-component business. But "fatigue" is loud discourse, not collapse - Tailwind is still ~12M weekly downloads. The people who actually switch go build-time, zero-runtime atomic CSS: UnoCSS and Panda CSS are the two most-watched alternatives, and in hands-on trials Panda keeps winning over vanilla-extract. StyleX (Meta's, behind Facebook/Instagram/Threads) is the credibility play but really a scale/maintainability tool, not a mass default. The loudest counter-movement is "you might not need a framework at all" - native nesting, container queries, and cascade layers now reproduce most of Tailwind's benefits with zero bundle bloat, with Lightning CSS (Rust) as the toolchain. Honest consensus: hybrid wins - Tailwind for ~90%, scoped/native CSS for the hard 10%. Tailwind v4's CSS-first @theme arguably absorbed the native-CSS argument rather than losing to it.

📱 Running image models locally on your own phone or laptop
The tools finally hide the stack. On Apple hardware, Draw Things is the default answer because it's "no terminal, no Python, no quantization flags" - a native Metal engine that runs FLUX.2, SDXL, SD 3.5, Qwen-Image on both Mac and iPhone, ~20-40% faster than ComfyUI on Apple Silicon. On Android the real tool is Local Dream, which leans on the Snapdragon Hexagon NPU (SD 1.5 broadly, SDXL only on 8 Gen 3+); the NPU, not model size, is the gating factor on a phone. The small-model conversation is now a VRAM-fit decision: SDXL for 8GB + the deepest LoRA ecosystem, FLUX.2 klein (4B, Apache 2.0) and Alibaba's Z-Image Turbo for sub-second speed, Qwen-Image when you need legible text. Image gen is increasingly bundled into local-first "your device, your data" suites (Off Grid, QVAC) rather than sold standalone. The honest caveat from the month's biggest thread: "local models are good now" was benchmarked on a 64GB Mac - and half of everyone has 16GB or less.
