What I learned:

**Nintendo's 2026 crackdown went from "two emulators" to "all of them"** - After the 2024 Yuzu settlement (a [$2.4M payout](https://www.chiplawgroup.com/nintendo-reaches-2-4-million-settlement-against-emulator-company/) that killed the project) and Ryujinx's quiet shutdown, the community assumed the forks would carry the torch. In February 2026 Nintendo fired DMCA notices at roughly a dozen Switch emulators and forks on GitHub at once - Citron, Eden, Kenji-NX, MeloNX, Pine, Pomelo, Ryubing, Ryujinx, Skyline, Sudachi, Sumi, Suyu, and Yuzu - per [Notebookcheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/Nintendo-sends-mass-DMCA-notices-to-Switch-emulators-on-GitHub-including-Yuzu-fork-Citron.1226292.0.html) and [NintendoEverything](https://nintendoeverything.com/nintendo-switch-emulator-takedown-github/). The legal theory is the same one that worked against Yuzu: not that the emulator code is infringing, but that it circumvents Nintendo's encryption under the DMCA - so the cleanly reverse-engineered code is "likely legal" yet still gets pulled.

**The community line is "emulation is legal, the crackdown is about keys"** - The most-shared social take in-window was the nuance, not the outrage. On X, [@picopicogamer](https://x.com/picopicogamer/status/2069697536575815877) (14 likes) summarized it plainly: Nintendo "took down one switch emulator which used stolen code... There was nothing they could do regarding the other emulator because it was clean. They made an offer to the devs and the devs took the deal." That maps to the consensus across [GBAtemp](https://gbatemp.net/threads/nintendo-issues-dmca-takedown-notices-to-ryujinx-forks-on-github.667947/) and the legal explainers: emulators are lawful, downloading ROMs you don't own is not, and the DMCA's Section 1201 anti-circumvention clause is the real chokepoint.

**Preservation lost in Brussels and won in California in the same month** - June 2026 was a split decision. The EU Commission declined to force publishers to keep purchased games playable after server shutdown, despite the [Stop Killing Games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stop_Killing_Games) campaign gathering ~1.3 million verified signatures, per [PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/gaming-industry/the-stop-killing-games-movement-hits-another-major-milestone-as-a-game-preservation-bill-passes-california-state-assembly-vote/). But the California Assembly passed the Protect Our Games Act (AB 1921) 43-16, which would require 60 days' notice before killing online-dependent games plus an offline mode, community-server path, or refund, per [Inven Global](https://www.invenglobal.com/articles/22330/stop-killing-games-movement-gains-momentum-california-assembly-passes-game-protection-bill). The [Video Game History Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Video_game_preservation)'s Digital Library (30,000+ files) keeps growing, but still operates under the shadow of Section 1201.

**Browser and WebAssembly emulators quietly routed around the takedown problem** - The structural shift is that emulation no longer needs an install, a binary, or an app-store listing that Nintendo can DMCA. [EmulatorJS](https://github.com/emulatorjs/emulatorjs) and webretro compile existing libretro cores (Snes9x, FCEUmm, mGBA) to WebAssembly via Emscripten and run them as an embeddable JS library off a CDN - so the emulator is just a webpage. The [Internet Archive](https://blog.archive.org/2018/02/13/emulation-in-the-browser-adds-webassembly/) now runs the majority of its emulated platforms in WASM, putting thousands of vintage titles one click away. The catch noted in mid-2026 coverage: WebGPU and SharedArrayBuffer remain native-only, so Chromium browsers still outperform everyone else for heavier cores.

**The "they delete it, it forks, repeat" loop is the real story** - Even with a 12-target sweep, the ecosystem kept moving: Eden, a Yuzu derivative that launched in pre-alpha in May 2025, became the first Switch emulator accepted onto Google Play, with a January 2026 release adding macOS and Android x86_64 support, per [RetroHandheldHQ](https://retrohandheldhq.com/posts/switch-emulator-2026/). The pattern - settlement, shutdown, fork, takedown, re-fork - is exactly why [Prism News](https://www.prismnews.com/hobbies/retro-game-emulation/yuzus-shutdown-reshapes-switch-emulation-forks-and) frames Yuzu's death as reshaping rather than ending Switch emulation.

KEY PATTERNS from the research:
1. Nintendo's 2026 escalation is breadth, not novelty - one Feb DMCA wave hit ~12 emulators/forks at once, per [Notebookcheck](https://www.notebookcheck.net/Nintendo-sends-mass-DMCA-notices-to-Switch-emulators-on-GitHub-including-Yuzu-fork-Citron.1226292.0.html)
2. The legal fight is about encryption circumvention (DMCA §1201), not emulator code - clean reverse-engineering is still "likely legal" per [GBAtemp](https://gbatemp.net/threads/nintendo-issues-dmca-takedown-notices-to-ryujinx-forks-on-github.667947/)
3. Preservation policy diverged hard in June 2026: EU declined Stop Killing Games, California passed AB 1921, per [PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/gaming-industry/the-stop-killing-games-movement-hits-another-major-milestone-as-a-game-preservation-bill-passes-california-state-assembly-vote/)
4. WebAssembly turns the emulator into an un-installable webpage - [EmulatorJS](https://github.com/emulatorjs/emulatorjs) and the [Internet Archive](https://blog.archive.org/2018/02/13/emulation-in-the-browser-adds-webassembly/) make takedown-by-binary obsolete
5. The fork-takedown-refork loop persists - Eden reached Google Play in Jan 2026 even as siblings were DMCA'd, per [RetroHandheldHQ](https://retrohandheldhq.com/posts/switch-emulator-2026/)
