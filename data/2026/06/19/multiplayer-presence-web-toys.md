What I learned:

**Cursor Camp is the toy that made everyone get it** - Neal Agarwal's [Cursor Camp](https://x.com/nealagarwal/status/2049503458844200992) (out April 29) drops you and a crowd of strangers onto an illustrated campsite as bare mouse cursors - no chat, no usernames, no profiles - and people are genuinely moved by it. [Aftermath](https://aftermath.site/cursor-camp-impressions/) nailed the feeling: "there's a strange comfort in being surrounded by other live cursors," because "you get the sense that there are real people on the other end of each pointer, doing exactly what you're doing." Reviewers keep reaching for Journey and Club Penguin to describe it, per [PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/games/mmo/the-creator-of-infinite-craft-has-a-new-game-where-you-explore-a-secret-filled-social-hub-as-your-mouse-cursor/).

**The magic word everyone uses is "vibe," not "feature"** - the [PartyKit Cursor Party](https://blog.partykit.io/posts/cursor-party/) team, who ship the open-source script that adds live cursors to any site, put it bluntly: multiplayer cursors "aren't the most sophisticated way to share presence but they are super effective," and once you realize "just behind the glass, there are other people and we can be together - well, that's a vibe." Their own GitHub README answers its own "Why?" with one word: ["Vibes."](https://github.com/partykit/cursor-party) That's the whole pitch, and it works.

**Presence without conversation is the actual feature** - what people love is the absence of the usual social-media machinery. No followers, no persistence, no performance - just the raw signal that you are not alone on this page. [Matt Webb's Interconnected](https://interconnected.org/home/2024/09/05/cursor-party) frames it as "every webpage deserves to be a place," and says seeing live cursors makes other websites feel impoverished by comparison. [Boing Boing](https://boingboing.net/2026/05/05/cursor-camp-is-a-wholesome-no-chat-online-hangout-spot.html) called Cursor Camp a "wholesome no-chat online hangout spot," which captures why it reads as comforting rather than chaotic.

**Wordless collaboration is where the warmth comes from** - the surprise hook is that people piece out what to do together, silently. In Cursor Camp strangers collaboratively build a fire and make mushroom stew with zero direct communication, per [PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/games/mmo/the-creator-of-infinite-craft-has-a-new-game-where-you-explore-a-secret-filled-social-hub-as-your-mouse-cursor/). The PartyKit crew describe the same thing on the open web as "serendipitous back-and-forths" - you wiggle your cursor, a stranger dances back, and that tiny unprompted reciprocity is the payoff.

**It's landing as a counter-move to the loneliness internet** - the most quietly telling signal in the data is [@nicole_ripka](https://x.com/nicole_ripka/status/2057888071555645833) writing that "more technology should create intimacy, not just scale," with a kid spotted "playing Cursor Camp at the dinner table." That's the cultural read: these toys feel magical because they reintroduce ambient human presence to a web that mostly optimized it away. On the builder side, presence is also going mainstream as table stakes - [@aayushman2703](https://x.com/aayushman2703/status/2064709405015495114) drew 1,200+ likes pitching a multiplayer site builder "where your whole team co-edits live and visitors see your published page being built."

**The infrastructure to make it yourself is right there** - this isn't just Neal's magic; it's a copy-pasteable category now. [partykit/cursor-party](https://github.com/partykit/cursor-party) lets you add multiplayer cursors to any site with a single script tag, and [liveblocks/liveblocks](https://github.com/liveblocks/liveblocks) (4.6K stars) sells the managed "realtime infrastructure for multiplayer apps" version. The barrier to making your own shared-presence toy collapsed, which is why the genre is spreading.

KEY PATTERNS from the research:
1. Presence beats interaction - just seeing live cursors is enough; the magic is "real people behind the glass," per [PartyKit](https://blog.partykit.io/posts/cursor-party/)
2. Stripping away chat/usernames/profiles is a feature, not a limitation - "wholesome no-chat hangout," per [Boing Boing](https://boingboing.net/2026/05/05/cursor-camp-is-a-wholesome-no-chat-online-hangout-spot.html)
3. Wordless collaboration (building fires, making stew together silently) is where the warmth lives, per [PC Gamer](https://www.pcgamer.com/games/mmo/the-creator-of-infinite-craft-has-a-new-game-where-you-explore-a-secret-filled-social-hub-as-your-mouse-cursor/)
4. People read it as an antidote to scale-without-intimacy - "technology should create intimacy, not just scale," per [@nicole_ripka](https://x.com/nicole_ripka/status/2057888071555645833)
5. "Every webpage deserves to be a place" - live cursors make the web feel alive again, per [Interconnected](https://interconnected.org/home/2024/09/05/cursor-party)
6. The category is now DIY - one script tag via [partykit/cursor-party](https://github.com/partykit/cursor-party) or managed infra via [Liveblocks](https://github.com/liveblocks/liveblocks)
